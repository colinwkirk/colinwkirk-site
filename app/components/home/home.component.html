About Me

I am a jack-of-all-trades software engineer. Give me any problem, in any language or stack, and I will work out a solution.

I started out working primarily with Java, C/C++, and Python. Throughout my years of experience I have also learned JavaScript/TypeScript (especially Node/Express and Angular), Groovy, Gosu, BASH, and even some Lisp, in environments ranging from embedded systems shops within the Department of Defense using Waterfall/Spiral SDLCs to Agile SaaS shops; I will continue to learn more languages and technologies as they are needed.

I've also filled a lot of different roles. I'm the truest of full-stack developers: I've been a systems/network administrator, QA engineer, backend engineer, integration developer, and I've done some front end/GUI work.

No matter what the problem is, I will not only solve the problem but will always find the best solution that will ensure optimization and increased process speed.

I have a unique personal history, as well. My first bachelor's degree is in art (with an emphasis in art history) with a fair amount of graphic design experience. I earned a second bachelor's degree in Aerospace Engineering with a minor in Computer Science, which led me to my current career (and yes, I do love Kerbal Space Program). I've been a lifelong musician and songwriter. I'm also a husband and proud father of two beautiful children, cyclist, martial artist, and gamer with a strong desire to travel with my family and see the world.

What's with the cow?

“Cosmic Cow” is the name of the cartoon drawn by the main character, Henry, in the 1980’s sitcom "Too Close For Comfort". I have no lasting memory of the show but Cosmic Cow has been my go-to username for all things internet since the days of dial-up when I would log into BBS’s and play “Legend of Red Dragon” and “Trade Wars.”

I’ve been doodling it for years and I recently decided to do put my graphic design skills to work and made it my logo!
